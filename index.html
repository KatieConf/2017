Call for Proposals

Closes 7th November, 2016 AOE

We want you to speak at WOOTCONF. We are looking for talks about things that make you want to nerd out and tell everyone about them. The new hotness in tech, the old faithful legacy, anything and everything is ok. The only limitation is: it must be about open source or open technology. It's no fun if you tell us all about your cool new toys if we can't play with them.

Submissions to the Call for Proposals can be made via the linux.conf.au website.

To submit a talk:

  • register and create a speaker profile
  • submit a proposal, ensuring you select "WOOTCONF"

We invite you to submit a proposal if you identify as a woman in a way that's significant to you.

If you would like any assistance with creating a proposal to submit, please don't hesistate to ask any one of the organisers, or make a note in the Private Abstract that you would like feedback.

The WOOTCONF schedule will be announced on or before 17 November 2016 (~60 days before the event)

About WOOTCONF

WOOTCONF, Women Of Open Tech, is a miniconf dedicated to allowing women to share their knowledge in relation to the future of open source in Australia in beyond.

Working on a foundation of previous linux.conf.au miniconfs AussieChix and Haecksen, WOOTCONF seeks to provide an environment allowing women to share their ideas and knowledge, with talks ranging from professional development, community and technical. WOOTCONF will include invited keynotes, and encourages new and upcoming speakers to propose and deliver talks. Mentoring by a number of recognised women in the open source community will be available for any speaker or attendee who wishes to use such help.

Women and allies are invited to attend the miniconf.
